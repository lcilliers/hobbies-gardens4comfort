calendula
===========
               
needles and pins calendula
----------------------------

This bushy annual will have lance-shaped, light green leaves with a rich aroma.  Double, orange flowerheads with incurved petals appear from late spring to autumn.
Good cutting flowers - strong stems
petals are edible and make colourful addition to salads.

Calendula and Tagetes (marigold) are often confused with each other. Calendula is a member of the daisy family and tagetes is a member of the sunflower family. Both plants have yellow flowers, but they are different shapes. Calendula flowers are round and have a lot of petals

Classification
----------------

 - Common Names: needles and pins calendula 
 - Family: officinalis 
 - Variety: needles and pins, cactus

 - Plant Type: annual 
 - Propagation: Seed 
 - Hardiness: Hardy H5-H1 (<-10) 
 - Flowering Season: Summer to autumn 

self sowing also - harvest small plants from garden and then replant them where they should be. Rake soil to provide firm, even surface. Sow in pre-watered soil; lightly cover with more soil; keep moist
thin seedlings to allow development and prevent overcrowding
allow 30cm between plants
For winter and early spring flowering as pot plants, sow in a cool greenhouse in late summer
