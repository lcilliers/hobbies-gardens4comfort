Amaranthus
============

love-lies bleeding Amaranthus
-------------------------------

Amaranthus is a short-lived frost-tender plant that's both ornamental and edible.

An upright annual with ovate mid-green leaves and attractive deep red branched flower spikes over the summer months. Popular with flower arrangers and for summer bedding and easy to grow, 

Amaranthus looks dramatic and immensely showy with enormous tassel-like crimson, purple or green flowers, or brilliantly coloured foliage

Propagation Notes
-------------------

 - Plant Type: annual 
 - Propagation: Seed 
 - Hardiness: Tender H7 - H10 (+0) 
 - Flowering Season: Summer to autumn 

Red
.5-1m
Full sun
It is best to sow grain Amaranths just beneath the soil surface in the spring, after the last frost. Use a spacing of 20 to 30 cm (8 to 12 inches) for smaller species and up to 50 cm (20 inches) apart for larger species Amaranthus Plants prefer partly shady to full sunlight conditions.

Sow under glass grow in loam-based compost (JI No2) in full light. 
Suggested planting locations and garden types. Cottage and informal garden. 
Pests. May be susceptible to aphids.
Diseases. May be susceptible to a virus.
