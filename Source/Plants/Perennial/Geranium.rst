geranium
----------
Compact, carpeting, rhizomatous. Flowers, small in pairs or clusters in early summer. Leaves aromatic, semi-evergreen. Grow in almost any moist or dry soil, in sun or semi-shade. Makes excellent grown cover.

Dusty cranesbill, Mourning widow
----------------------------------
Clump-forming, rhizomatous, White flowers from late spring to summer.
This species is an evergreen perennial with 7-9 lobed pale green leaves. The flowers are produced in cymes of white, deep purple, black small blooms in the spring to summer.

Geranium F1 Hybrid
--------------------
This exceptional variety, Geranium F1 'Best Red' outperformed all others in our trials. The uniform, well branched plants become smothered in large, shapely scarlet flowers that retain their form, without shattering as they fade. These vigorous pelargonium plants are guaranteed to create a top performance in beds, borders and containers. Height: 45cm (18"). Spread: 35cm (14").

Classification
----------------

 - Common Names: Geranium F1 Hybrid  - Family:   - Variety: F1 Hybrid

Propagation Notes
-------------------

 - Plant Type: annual  - Propagation: Seed  - Hardiness: Semi Hardy H6 (-10)  - Flowering Season: Early summer 
Our garden ready plants are selected for garden performance, straight from the nursery. We send them to you 'green' ahead of flowering (but please note some varieties may show a few blooms), which means plants will establish quickly and all their energy goes into producing better roots, so they produce more flowers throughout the season.
Although technically perennial, Pelargoniums are only half hardy and therefore normally treated as annuals in the UK. However, with care, they may be overwintered in a frost free greenhouse.

Geranium wild
---------------
eranium maculatum, the wild geranium, spotted geranium, or wood geranium, is a perennial plant native to woodland in eastern North America, from southern Manitoba and southwestern Quebec south to Alabama and Georgia and west to Oklahoma and South Dakota.

Classification
----------------
