Digitalis
===========
               
foxgloves
-----------
D. purpurea is a biennial or short-lived perennial, freely self-sowing, with a rosette of softly hairy, oval leaves and tall, one-sided spires of pendant, tubular, bright rosy-purple flowers 6cm long, spotted within, in summer

Classification
----------------

 - Common Names: foxgloves  - Family: Plantaginaceae  - Variety: Purpurea

Propagation Notes
-------------------

 - Plant Type: Bi-annual  - Propagation: Seed  - Hardiness: Tender H7 - H10 (+0)  - Flowering Season: Mid summer 
Grow in almost any soil,but avoid very wet or very dry situations. Prefers a humus-rich soil in partial shade but will grow in full sun. Deadhead after flowering to avoid problems with excess numbers of seedlings.

Foxgloves white Digitalis
---------------------------
A biennial or short-lived perennial, with softly hairy leaves and tall, one-sided spires of pendant, tubular, creamy-white flowers 6cm long

Classification
----------------

 - Common Names: Foxgloves white Digitalis  - Family:   - Variety: Purpurea f albiflora

Propagation Notes
-------------------

 - Plant Type: Bi-annual  - Propagation: Seed  - Hardiness: Hardy H5-H1 (<-10)  - Flowering Season: Summer to autumn 
1,5m; full sun, semi shade
sow july, Aug and plant out Sept, Oct
Grow Digitalis purpurea in moist but well-drained soil in sun to shade. Allow plants to seed before cutting back the flowering stem – this may encourage a second flush of blooms to grow.
               
Foxgloves, large yellow Digitalis
-----------------------------------
A clump-forming perennial with dark, evergreen foliage and erect spires of tubular, creamy-yellow flowers 4-5cm in length

Classification
----------------

 - Common Names: Foxgloves, large yellow Digitalis  - Family:   - Variety: Grandiflora

Propagation Notes
-------------------

 - Plant Type: Perennial  - Propagation: Seed  - Hardiness: Hardy H5-H1 (<-10)  - Flowering Season: Summer to autumn 
full sun, semi shade,full shade
.5-1m
Digitalis grandiflora has been awarded the Royal Horticultural Society’s Award of Garden Merit (AGM). Sowing: Sow indoors in late winter to spring or sow directly outdoors in late summer to autumn Sow seeds on the surface of a peaty soil. Do not cover or bury seeds as the seed needs light to germinate, just press seeds lightly into the earth.
               
Smooth foxgloves Digitalis laevigata
--------------------------------------
A deciduous perennial, forming a basal clump of oval, dark green leaves, with erect leafy stems bearing loose racemes of brown-veined, light yellow flowers in summer

Classification
----------------

 - Common Names: Smooth foxgloves Digitalis laevigata  - Family:   - Variety: laevigata

Propagation Notes
-------------------

 - Plant Type: Perennial  - Propagation: Seed  - Hardiness: Hardy H5-H1 (<-10)  - Flowering Season: Mid summer 
yellow, brown 1m full sun, semi shade
The seeds of Digitalis can be planted out in the middle of spring or autumn. They should be sown on the surface at about 50 cm apart. They like to grow in the sun but it is probably best to grow them in an area that is partially shaded in the afternoon
