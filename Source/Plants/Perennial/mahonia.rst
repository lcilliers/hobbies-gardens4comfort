mahonia

Creeping barberry
-------------------
These are evergreen shrubs which have short racemes of fragrant, rounded, bell-shaped, yellow flowers on tall species; some cultivars have deep fissured bark. They are fully to half hardy and will prefer shade or semi-shade with fertile well drained but not too dry soil.  You can propagate the species by leaf-bud or semi-ripe cuttings in the summer or by seed in the autumn.

his shrub grows to about 45cm, It has a creeping habit this makes it  useful as groundcover, The leaves are green, with spiny leaflets, they often turn a reddish shade in cold winters. The flowers are small, fragrant and yellow they are borne in the spring they are followed by  globular fruit.
