begonia semperflorens fibrous
-------------------------------
Bushy, usually compact, fibrous rooted evergreen perennial hybrids, derived from B cucullata. They are grown for their leaves and flowers. Flower well in partial shade, but tolerate all but direct overhead sunlight. Keep container plants just moist and well ventilated in winter

Classification
----------------

 - Common Names: begonia semperflorens fibrous  - Family: semperflorens  - Variety:

begonia tuberous
------------------
Cultivars which are usually derived from Begonia × tuberhybrida are a favourite of gardeners for their bright colours and long flowering season. Male and female flowers are borne separately on the same plant, the male flowers being the showiest. Selections and hybrids of B. boliviensis such as Begonia ‘Million Kisses’ are also available and are excellent free flowering plants for baskets and pots.

Classification
----------------

 - Common Names: begonia tuberous  - Family: semperflorens  - Variety: tuberhybrida
